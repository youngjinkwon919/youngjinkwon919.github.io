    • Abstract: Narrative visualization is a series of interactive visualizations sequenced in order to maximize storytelling potential. Although narrative visualization is being actively utilized by journalists and storytellers, there lack theoretical, empirical studies on how diverse visualization stories affect reading experience. This study characterizes narrative visualization elements through the lens of visual narratives, and investigates the mechanism of the author and readerâ€™s interplay inside the â€™visual storyâ€™. This study views level of interactivity and author guidance to be crucial determinants of narrative visualization, and evaluates how the determinants affect visualization interpretation and engagement by a 2X2 between-subject experiment. Studying unique storytelling features of narrative visualization and quantitatively measuring reading experience will provide insight to both visualization designers and authoring tool developers.
